# Reverse-Actualization Protocol

## What It Is
Reverse-actualization is a formal methodology for strategic planning. Instead of planning forward from today, you imagine the fully realized future state and work backward to identify what to build NOW.

## The 7 Horizons

### Near-Term (Concrete)
- **1 year**: Specific features, metrics, milestones. High confidence (0.9).
- **3 years**: Market position, user growth, ecosystem effects. Medium confidence (0.7).
- **5 years**: Industry impact, competitive landscape. Lower confidence (0.5).

### Mid-Term (Directional)
- **10 years**: Cultural shifts that make the technology inevitable. Compass bearing (0.3).
- **25 years**: How daily life has changed. The technology is invisible — like electricity.

### Long-Term (North Star)
- **50 years**: Trajectory check. Are we still heading in the right direction?
- **100 years**: The ultimate purpose. What does this technology mean for humanity?

## Horizon Focus: Users, Not Features
Each horizon focuses on **users and their lives** — how the technology entangles in their day-to-day cycles. Not feature lists. Not technical specifications. Human stories.

## The Backward Pass
After all 7 horizons are explored, the agent works backward:
1. From 100yr: What is the North Star?
2. From 50yr: What trajectory must we maintain?
3. From 25yr: What cultural shifts must we anticipate?
4. From 10yr: What does "invisible technology" look like?
5. From 5yr: What ecosystem must we build?
6. From 3yr: What market position must we hold?
7. From 1yr: What specific features must we ship?

Each step extracts **turning points** — the critical decisions between horizons.

## Compass Bearings vs Maps
- **≤10 years**: Maps. Concrete features, specific dates, measurable outcomes.
- **≥25 years**: Compass bearings. Directional guidance, not specific features. The landscape will change.

Key insight: When 25 years becomes 5 years (because time passed), the system will already have simulated the new 25 years out since it was 45 years out and part of the 50-year and 100-year simulations. The compass bearings sharpen into maps over time.

## Synthesis Outputs
1. **Critical Decisions**: The 5 most important decisions to make NOW
2. **Roadmap**: 12-month concrete action plan
3. **Baton Handoff**: What to remember when you return in 6 months
4. **Schemas**: Logic-graphs, spreadsheet agents, langchain flows, API endpoints
5. **A/B Branches**: Optimization experiments with success metrics

## Multi-Model Protocol
- **Phase 1 (Understanding)**: 3 providers analyze repo independently
- **Phase 2 (Horizons)**: ALL providers simulate each horizon (parallel)
- **Phase 3 (Synthesis)**: 3 best providers synthesize backward
- **Phase 4 (Documentation)**: 2 providers generate handoff docs

Each model's output is recorded for effectiveness tracking.

## Recursion
The process is recursive. When you return in 6 months:
1. Load previous report from KV
2. Run RA again with new context
3. Compare new horizons with old compass bearings
4. Sharpen: what was compass is now map
5. Extend: simulate new 100-year horizon (now 99.5 years out)

The accumulation of reports IS the crystallization graph.
